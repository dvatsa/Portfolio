About me

I am a senior at BASIS Scottsdale High School with a love for research, computing, social activism, and dance. I have over 10 years of extensive training in Indian Classical Dance and have used dance as a vehicle for my social activism and gender equality campaigns. When I am not dancing, I like to explore the applications of computer science to medical research. Over the past three years, I have worked on a computational biology project to identify effective chemotherapy regimens at the Harvard Medical School's Boston Children's Hospital, pursued computational neuroscience research at the Barrow Neurological Institute, and conducted independent research on the impact of social media on gender perceptions.

Research Experience

My inquisitive nature and fascination with science led me to conduct my first research project in middle school. In high school, I have had the honor of interning at formal research institutions such as Barrow Neurological Insitute and Boston Children's Hospital through selective application processes.

Barrow Neurological Institute

I was selected as 1 of 7 students in Arizona to be a research intern at BNI as part of the Scientific Enrichment Program for Students in 10th grade. Under the direction of Dr. Leslie Baxter, I have been working in the Neuroimaging Lab, focused on exploring funtional connectivity for aging autistic patients. I am currently researching computational machine learning methods to provide greater insights on the compounding effects of aging on these individuals.

Boston Children's Hospital

As a 2016 Research Science Institute (RSI) Scholar, I was selected to intern at Harvard Medical School - Boston Children's Hospital the summer of my junior year as 1 of 82 high school juniors around the world. RSI is a six-week fully paid research program held at MIT. At BCH, I worked under the mentorship of Dr. Gil Alterovitz to develop a computational model to identify effective chemotherapy regimens.

Dance Experience

Since the age of five, I have trained in the Classical Indian dance form Bharatnatyam. In 2014, after 10 years of training, I had my Arangetram (graduation performance). I enjoy performing and showcasing my art at cultural programs, the Talking Stick Sports Arena, annual programs, and community gala events. My two biggest dance accomplishments have been AZA and my Arangetram.

AZA

I co-founded AZA with 3 other dance partners in the summer of ninth grade. AZA, meaning hope in Sanskrit, is a dance production that portrays the various social issues related to gender disparities that impact our society. Each choreography highlights a different issue such as educational inequities and abusive relationships. We leveraged our passion for dance as a medium to promote social awareness. AZA is an annual performance.

Tempe Center for the Arts

Our first performance was premiered at the Tempe Center for the Arts in January, 2015 to a fully sold-out audience of over 600 people. The show raised $7,000 for She's the First to sponsor the education of six girls in Uganda, Ethiopia, Peru, India, and Guatemala.

American Muslim Women's Association (AMWA)

Our second performance was at the AMWA Annual Gala Event in February, 2016. We raised awareness of gender inequities in front of an audience of over 250 people. AMWA's mission is to improve the economic status of women from all ethnic and religious backgrounds residing in our community.

Arangetram

An Arangetram marks the completion of the initial dance training for a student, and it translates to "ascending the stage." My Arangetram was a 3 hour solo debut performance at the Mesa Arts Center in June, 2014. Under the tutelage of Mrs. Nita Mallya, this performance marked the culmination of 10 years of rigorous training. Various live musical instruments and vocalists accompanied my performance.

Programming and STEM Outreach

Technology plays a significant role in many of my academic and creative endeavors. I first became engaged in technology through Photoshop and creative graphics design in middle school. Ever since, I have self-taught various programming languages such as Python, R, MATLAB, and HTML. The tech initiative that I'm most proud of is STEMxCHANGE, a nonprofit to empower other youth through a STEM education.

STEMxCHANGE

I founded STEMxCHANGE as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Our mission is to empower youth through free STEM education. We have partnered with after-school programs and received a $3,000 grant from NCWIT to promote our program in the community. Currently, we have 5 active chapters.

Gender Equality Campaign

I have promoted gender equality within my community through dance and STEM outreach. In 10th grade, I founded the BASIS Scottsdale Chapter of She's the First and was awarded Townsend National Chapter Leader of the Year in 2015.

She’s the First BASIS Scottsdale has fundraised over $8,000 to sponsor the education of eight girls around the world. Our overall goal is to help unlock the potential of our world by providing as many people as possible with educational opportunities. Within our community, we also host an annual bake sale and awareness workshops about the problem of inequality in society.
