About me

I am a senior at BASIS Scottsdale High School with a love for research, computing, social activism, and dance. I have over 10 years of extensive training in Indian Classical Dance and have used dance as a vehicle for my social activism and gender equality campaigns. When I am not dancing, I like to explore the applications of computer science to medical research. Over the past three years, I have conducted independent research on the impact of social media on gender perceptions, pursued computational neuroscience research at the Barrow Neurological Institute, and worked on a computational biology project in chemotherapy at the Harvard Medical School's Boston Children's Hospital.

Research Experience

I have taken part in scientific research since middle school. My fascination in science led me to conduct independent research studies through tenth grade. Since then, I have had the honor of being selected through selective application processes to intern at formal research institutions such as the Barrow Neurological Insitute and the Boston Children's Hospital through the Research Science Insitute (RSI).

Barrow Neurological Institute

I was selected as 1 of 7 students in Arizona to be a research intern at the institute as part of the Scientific Enrichment Program for Students in 10th grade. I have been working in the Neuroimaging Lab under the direction of Dr. Leslie Baxter ever since. My research is focused on exploring funtional connectivity for aging autistic patients. I am currently implementing machine learning to the study.

Boston Children's Hospital

I was given the opportunity to intern at Harvard Medical School - Boston Children's Hospital the summer of my junior year. I was selected as 1 of 82 high school juniors around the world to attend MIT's RSI, a six-week fully paid research program. I worked under the direction of Dr. Gil Alterovitz to develop a computational model for chemotherapy regimens.

Dance Experience

I have been training in the Classical Indian Dance, Bharatnatyam, since the age of 5. I have performed in cultural programs, Phoenix Mercury pre-game shows, dance school annual programs, and other events. My two biggest accomplishments have been AZA and my Arangetram performance.

AZA

AZA, meaning hope in Sanskrit, is a dance production that was co-founded by me and 3 other dance partners to explore the social issues related to gender dispartity that impact our society through the medium of dance.

Tempe Center for the Arts

Our first performance was premiered at the Tempe Center for the Arts in January, 2015 to a fully sold-out audience of over 600 people and we raised $7,000 to sponsor the education of 6 girls in Uganda, Ethiopia, Peru, India, and Guatemala.

American Muslim Women's Association (AMWA)

Our second performance at the AMWA Annual Gala Event on Feb 2016 to raise awareness of gender inequities in front of an audience of over 250 people. AMWA's mission is to improve the economic status of women from all ethnic and religious backgrounds residing in our community.

Arangetram

An Arangetram marks the completion of the initial dance training for a student, and it translates to "ascending the stage." It is a solo debut performance lasting 3 hours. It required 10 years of rigorous training, commitment, and hard work. Various live musical instruments and vocalists accompanied my performance. I performed on June 1, 2014 at the Mesa Arts Center

Gender Equality Campaign

I have promoted gender equality within my community through dance and STEM outreach. Also founded the BASIS Scottsdale Chapter of She's the First in 10th grade. Awarded Townsend National Chapter Leader of the Year in 2015.

The BASIS Scottsdale chapter of She’s the First aims to raise awareness about the problem of inequality in society. Additionally, we fundraise in order to sponsor a girl’s education in a developing country. We also aim to provide opportunities locally through workshops. We want to create a strong sense of community to help promote equality and opportunity. Our overall goal is to help unlock the potential of our world by providing as many people as possible with educational opportunities.
